Gender Equality

Gender equality is not only a fundamental human right, but a necessary foundation for a peaceful, prosperous and sustainable world. There has been progress over the last decades, but the world is not on track to achieve gender equality by 2030.

Women and girls represent half of the world’s population and therefore also half of its potential. But gender inequality persists everywhere and stagnates social progress.

How much progress have we made?

Some progress has been made in gender equality, like fewer child marriages and more women in politics. However, true equality is still out of reach, and the COVID-19 pandemic has made things even harder for women and girls.

Why should gender equality matter to me?

No matter where you live, gender equality is a basic human right. It helps make society better by reducing poverty and improving health, education, safety, and well-being for both girls and boys.

What can we do?

If you’re a girl, you can stay in school, encourage your classmates to do the same, and stand up for your right to access healthcare and support.

If you’re a woman, you can challenge hidden biases and stereotypes that make it harder for women to have the same opportunities as men.

If you’re a man or a boy, you can work together with women and girls to create a fairer world and build healthy, respectful relationships.
